### Recommendation: Malai Broccoli

> Malai Broccoli is exactly that melt-in-your-mouth Indian starter, and the Malai Broccoli recipe on My Ginger Garlic Kitchen uses your broccoli, cashews, and mint straight through.

This dish is built on a creamy cashew marinade studded with broccoli florets and finished with mint, which is precisely what you have on hand. The My Ginger Garlic Kitchen version calls it melt-in-your-mouth by name, so it matches both your craving and your pantry almost exactly. You will just need to round out the dairy side of the marinade.

Missing:
- [essential] hung curd or cream cheese — This is what gives the marinade its rich, clingy texture. If you don't have either, thick full fat yogurt strained for an hour works as a stand in.
- [essential] fresh cream — Adds the malai richness. A splash of full fat coconut cream can substitute if you're out of dairy cream.
- [nice_to_have] processed cheese — Helps the marinade coat and char nicely, but you can skip it and lean more on the cashew paste and cream.
- [nice_to_have] skewers or a grill pan — Useful for getting the classic charred tikka look, though a hot oven or air fryer works too.

Primary sources:
- Malai Broccoli — mygingergarlickitchen.com — https://www.mygingergarlickitchen.com/malai-broccoli/

How to use: Follow the Malai Broccoli recipe on My Ginger Garlic Kitchen as your base, it already builds the cashew and mint marinade you want. If you'd rather skip dairy cheese and cream entirely, borrow the cashew paste as dairy substitute idea from the Malai Broccoli restaurant style page on J Cooking Odyssey, and use its mint chutney pairing suggestion for serving.

Alternatives:
- Malai Broccoli (restaurant style) (jcookingodyssey.com) — Better pick if you want to go fully cashew based with little to no dairy, since it explicitly swaps in cashew paste. — https://www.jcookingodyssey.com/malai-broccoli/
- Malai Broccoli Kebabs (eathealthy365.com) — Good option if you're fine using mint only as a chutney garnish rather than folding it into the marinade itself. — https://eathealthy365.com/easy-creamy-malai-broccoli-kebabs/
- Malai Broccoli Tikka (cookinganytime.com) — Worth trying if you have an air fryer and want a lighter, crisper version of the same melt-in-your-mouth texture. — https://cookinganytime.com/malai-broccoli-tikka/
