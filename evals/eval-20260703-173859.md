> Tonight's the night for a creamy Greek yogurt chickpea curry — one pot, half an hour, and it puts both your yogurt and chickpeas to work.

You want to use up yogurt and chickpeas in a vegetarian dinner, and this curry is built around exactly that — a full cup of Greek yogurt stirred in for creaminess plus a can of chickpeas as the star. It's a 30-minute one-pot meal, so cleanup stays easy on a weeknight. The Plant Based School page keeps everything meatless from the start, so you don't have to make any swaps.

Missing:
- [essential] onion, garlic, and ginger — The aromatic base; if you're short on fresh ginger, a pinch of ground works.
- [essential] curry powder or garam masala — The spine of the flavor — any warm curry blend you have will do.
- [essential] crushed tomatoes — Builds the sauce; a few tablespoons of tomato paste loosened with water can fill in.
- [nice_to_have] vegetable broth — Water plus a bit of salt works if you don't have any.
- [nice_to_have] rice or naan — For serving; skip if you'd rather eat it as a stew.

Primary sources:
- Greek Yogurt Chickpea Curry — theplantbasedschool.com — https://theplantbasedschool.com/greek-yogurt-chickpea-curry/

How to use: Follow the Greek Yogurt Chickpea Curry on The Plant Based School as your main recipe for the base, sauce, and timing. One tip worth borrowing from the Blessedish version: fold the yogurt in off the heat so it stays smooth and doesn't split — a small move that makes a big difference with dairy yogurt.
